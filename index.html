Country_GDP Visualisations

GDP is a key economic indicator that used to assess the economic performance and the size of a country's economy.
The "Country_GDP" dataset provides a comprehensive overview of countries and their economic indicators, making it a valuable resource for a wide range of analyses and investigations. With details on GDP figures from both the International Monetary Fund (IMF) and the United Nations (UN), population data, and GDP per capita, this dataset allows us to explore the economic landscape of nations worldwide.
By using GDP per capita as a metric, one can delve into income distribution and standards of living across countries.

International Monetary Fund (IMF) and United Nations (UN)

Comparing IMF_GDP and UN_GDP data can help identify discrepancies, potential areas of improvement, and provide a more accurate representation of the nation's economic health. IMF_GDP generally being higher than the UN_GDP for most countries.

GDP per capita and Population

The population and GDP per capita figures for these countries vary significantly, with some highly populous countries like China and India having relatively lower GDP per capita compared to countries with smaller populations like the United States, Switzerland, and Australia, which have much higher GDP per capita values.
